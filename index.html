The Environmentalist Movement

A Social Activism Movement That Changed America

What Are the Main Goals of This Movement?

Raise Public Awareness on the Topic About Environmental Damage

The movements main goal was to educate the American Public about pollution, pesticide damages, and ecological damage caused by industrialization

Address Public Health Concerns

Another goal to highlight the connection between environmental pollution and health damages in particular, water quality and the quality of the food you're eating.

Create a Shift in How the American Public Viewed Nature

Aimed to create a bond and a change in the way the American public viewed nature and to view it as a friend and treat it good.

Where/How Does the Movement Claim Change Is Necessary on This Topic?

1. Industrial Pollution Was Polluting Air and Water

Factories were releasing toxic chemicals into rivers and the atmosphere. This caused contamination and health problems in America.

2. Pesticides Were Harming Ecosystems

Rachel Carrison's "Silent Spring" exposed how pesticides were killing animals like birds and this was hurting the food chain of ecosystems.

3. Development of Communities Was Destroying Natural Habitats

Unchecked urban development was spreading fast as a wildfire this was damaging habitats and threatening biodiversity.

Acts/Key Events of the Movement

What events, activities, rallies, written word, debates, etc. took place on this topic?

1962

The Publication of "Silent Spring" by Rachel Carrison (1962)

Book written to expose the truth about pesticides and how it was damaging environments.

1969

Santa Barbara Oil Spill (January 1969)

A massive oil spill off the coast of Cali that released aprx 3 mil gallons of oil into the ocean killing thousands of sea birds and marine animals. This disaster gave the movement a thing to prove their points on and rally around.

1969

Cuyahoga River Fire (June 1969)

The river fire in Ohio caught fire due to extreme pollution becoming a symbol of how pollution can damage environments and people's health

1970

First Earth Day (April 22, 1970)

Aprx 20 million Americans participated in rallies, teachings and demonstrations across the country making it one of the largest organized protests in US history.

Effects/Significance of the Movement

What has changed because of the movement? Have any laws changed/passed from it? Who did/didn't support the movement?

1. Creation of the EPA in 1970

Pres Nixon established the Environmental Protection Agency to enforce environmental laws. This marked the first federal agency dedicated to protecting the environment and public health

2. Passage of the Landmark Environmental Legislation

The movement led to many laws including the Clean Air Act, Clean Water Act, Endangered Species Act, and the ban on DDT (a pesticide)

3. Shift in Public Consciousness

Businesses began facing penalties for damaging the environment, and the public awareness grew and the issues of the environment became a main priority to fix.

Connection to the Present Day

How can we tell this movement has/has not been successful? Is there anything today we can see is a direct outcome of the movement? Is the movement ongoing today?

1. Measurable Environmental Improvements

Air and water quality have improved drastically since the 1970s due to regulations established by the government because of this movement. Lead has been removed from gasoline, DDT was banned, many endangered species have been saved from extinction, and smog in cities have decreased significantly

2. Environment Protection Is Now a Main Priority

The EPA continues to operate, and enforces environmental law. Earth Day is celebrated world wide, and environmental education is a part of learning in schools now.

3. The Movement Still Continues Today

Today the environmental movement has expanded to address more things like climate change, renewable energy. Also the movement still continues with a girl named Gretta Thunberg leading the pack.
